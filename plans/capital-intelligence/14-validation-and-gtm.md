# 14 — Validation & Go-To-Market Plan

The build plan produces a demo; this doc plans how the demo becomes a
business. Kept deliberately lean — GTM hypotheses get revised by contact
with real advisors, so we plan the *learning machine*, not a fantasy funnel.

## 1. Target segment (initial hypothesis)

**Independent RIAs, $500M–$5B AUM, 3–25 advisors.** Why: they buy their own
tech (no wirehouse procurement), they feel the research/monitoring pain
without an in-house research department, and one CIO or managing partner can
say yes. Secondary: multi-family offices (the Grupo Vela archetype exists in
the seed for exactly this conversation).

Who we are NOT selling to first: wirehouses (procurement + compliance
timeline), solo advisors (<$100M — willingness to pay too low for the value
prop), hedge funds (different product, per 01 §10 that's a later application
of the engine).

## 2. Positioning

"Bloomberg costs $30k/seat and shows everyone the same firehose. CRMs track
what already happened. **We start from what your clients own** and tell you
what deserves your attention today — with the evidence trail to prove it."

Price-anchor frame: above YCharts/Koyfin-class research tools, far below
Bloomberg/FactSet, priced as intelligence-per-advisor, not data-per-seat.
**Pricing hypothesis to test (not announce): $750/advisor/month**, firm
minimum $2k/month, pilot discount 50% for design partners. Validate in
partner conversations before any public pricing.

## 3. Design partner program (3–5 firms)

- **Recruit** via the founder's existing wealth-management network (the
  transcript's origin conversations) + warm intros. Offer: heavy input on
  roadmap, 50% pilot pricing for year one, no data obligations in the demo
  phase (seeded data), CSV-import pilot in Phase 2 W3.
- **Cadence:** M2 demo (map only) → directional feedback on the signature
  screen. M4 demo (full loop on seed) → workflow validation: "would this
  change your Monday morning?" M6 demo (polished) → pilot letter-of-intent
  conversation.
- **Structured feedback:** every session scores the same 5 questions (1–5):
  would you log in daily / did the priorities feel right / did you trust the
  explanations / what would you pay / what's missing. Notes go in the
  planning repo; scores tracked demo-over-demo.

## 4. The three validation gates

| Gate | Evidence required | If it fails |
|---|---|---|
| G1 (after M2) | ≥3 of 5 partners say the exposure-first map is meaningfully different from tools they use | Revisit signature-screen design before investing in M3+ agents |
| G2 (after M4) | ≥2 partners say Today's priorities would change their actual week; explanations trusted (≥4/5) | Loop quality problem: tune agents/seed before building M5 breadth |
| G3 (after M6) | ≥2 pilot LOIs at (discounted) target pricing | Pricing/segment problem: revisit §1–2 before Phase 2 spend |

The gates deliberately mirror the engine's own philosophy: hypotheses with
evidence and explicit "what would change our view" triggers.

## 5. Metrics that matter (pilot phase)

Advisor weekly active rate · priorities acted on (task created/completed
from a Today item) · meeting-prep time self-reported before/after ·
evidence-drawer open rate (are they checking our work? — early trust
proxy, should start high and *decline* as trust builds) · "who should I
call today" answers rated useful. Vanity metrics we ignore: nodes in graph,
documents ingested, AI calls.

## 6. Competitive frame (for the deck, one line each)

Bloomberg/FactSet: data terminals, news-first, no client mapping. YCharts/
Koyfin: charts and screening, no intelligence loop. Wealth-tech AI copilots
(Jump, Zocks, FinMate-class): meeting transcription/CRM automation — they
automate *records* of the past; we organize *attention* on what's next.
Nitrogen/YCharts proposal tools: sales artifacts, not living intelligence.
Our wedge is the exposure-first loop + explainability; our moat compounds
via institutional memory (01 §8).

## 7. Sequencing with the build

GTM work runs in parallel with M3–M6 and needs ~½ day/week of founder time:
partner recruiting during M2–M3, demo cadence at M2/M4/M6, pricing
conversations only after G2. No public launch, website, or content motion
until G3 passes — the demo is the marketing until then.
